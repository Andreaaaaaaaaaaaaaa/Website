    When I was a wee boy I found a duck. A little duck. That day I became a father. I carried the duck home and told my mother that I was a old duck father. She said men couldn't be ducks and my dreams were crushed. So I opened a bakery when I was 30 years young. My wee duck, she died like me mum. Either way I had another duck. This one was little baby duck. Made of rubber and gold. It would never leave me, not like me first one. Not like me mother. Not like me father. Not like me wife. Anyway know I own a successful bakery. Don't need no people, I got me little duckey and I got me rum, don't need no wife
